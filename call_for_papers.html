Call for Papers

We invite submissions on privacy, memorization, legal perspective, and evaluation in foundation and agentic models.

Topics of Interest

  • Definitions of privacy for foundation models and agentic systems, including contextual, social, legal, and formal perspectives.
  • Memorization and model-mediated leakage in foundation models, including training-data extraction, membership inference, attribute inference, reconstruction attacks, and semantic disclosure of sensitive information.
  • Privacy attacks in agentic systems, including prompt and tool injection for memory extraction, cross-session leakage, nd multi-agent collusion.
  • Benchmarks, auditing frameworks, and realistic evaluations of privacy leakage and memorization.
  • Mitigations for privacy-sensitive AI systems, including access control, information-flow control, privacy filters, unlearning, red-teaming, and privacy-preserving tool use.
  • Formal guarantees and verifiable protections, including differential privacy, policy verification, formal methods, and the limits of guarantees for adaptive and tool-using systems.
  • Human-centered, legal, and governance perspectives on contextual privacy, consent, autonomy, acceptable information use, user expectations, accountability, and regulatory compliance.
  • Interactions between privacy and other system properties, including utility, personalization, safety, robustness, security, transparency, and deployability.
  • Social, economic, legal, and political consequences of privacy violations, including downstream harms from memorization, sensitive inference, cross-context information sharing, and autonomous decision-making.
